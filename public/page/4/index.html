Introduction to the Neurochemical Foundations of Learning and Memory for Non-Science Majors Jun 4, 2014 This first in a series of posts about the biology of learning and memory has been written plainly, with much care taken to ensure its general-audience accessibility. TL;DR: neurons communicate with other neurons by releasing chemicals called neurotransmitters onto them; these chemicals bind to receptors on the neighboring neurons. When a neuron repeatedly “fires” on another neuron, this triggers the latter to produce additional receptors and can even stimulate physical growth toward the former; thus, the latter becomes especially sensitive to the former and more likely to fire when the former does. ... ➦
